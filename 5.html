Major Ethical Concerns in Recommender Systems

Hallucination / Misinformation / Manipulation

  • False or misleading information caused by misinterpretation of relationships, probabilities or correlations, resulting in illogical or misleading information
  • Deepfakes, audio and video manipulation could also be done

Bias / Discrimination

  • Without human-in-the-loop or guidance from supervised/reinforcement learnings, LLMs may (mis)interpret correlations or observe unethical actions and output as results, causing bias or discrimination to exist; Examples include Apple’s providing ladies lower credit limits than gentlemen without proper reason

Opacity / Black-box

  • Unknown algorithms or mechanisms due to “Black-box” issue, causing the users or developers to only recognize the input/output; Such opacity in ML/LLMs may result in low transparency, leading to unpredictable or uninterpretable logic

Lack of Robustness / Hack

  • LLMs consist of levels of data structuring and deep learning layers or thought processes, which without proper layers or algorithms to conduct adversarial training or noise injection, there will be perturbations or attacks that either destroy, info-steal or manipulate the LLMs
  • However, robustness may come at a cost of higher overfitting or worsened performance, and is also requiring higher computational cost

Unjustified usage of sources / Privacy and Data Usage

  • Many sources or information used to train LLMs may be obtained through opaque or restrictive “Term of Usages”, or even are obtained without the users or producers’ consents, leading to a potential intellectual property infringement
  • Some other information may also include personal information or sensitive data which might result in privacy or confidential breaches after regeneration of outputs after training the model

Economic / Social Impact

  • LLM technology can lead to repetitive or brainstorming jobs being removed as LLMs can either conduct or generate such items; Also, existing environment may be impacted by LLMs as seen from above part where companies have to adapt LLMs into daily usage, leading to new skillset or resource requirements needed in the market
