How Big Companies Use Recommender Systems?

Moving on to the practical use of large language models (LLMs) in recommender systems, the major distinction from traditional recommendation systems is how the system understands users' needs and provides the most suitable products based on their filters. LLMs overcome limitations by using keywords for search and create a more natural shopping environment for users. We utilize Taobao and HKTV Mall as examples of the practical use.

Taobao

The most popular and largest e-commerce shopping platform in Mainland China. Instead of just using the common “Search” function, they also provided the shopping assistant. Users can communicate with the AI shopping assistant called “Taobao Wenwen”. It is integrated into the search interface and interacts with users while they browse Taobao.

Example:

Assume the user is preparing for an upcoming camping trip.

  • Traditional recommendation system: Search for the keyword “Camping Tent”
  • Using LLMs recommendation system: User: “What should I prepare for a camping trip with 2 friends?”, then the Taobao Wenwen will showcase the essential items people need for camping.

It not only shows how Taobao uses LLMs to search for complex commands, but also demonstrates the accuracy of matching against billions of products and shops in Taobao's backend database to provide the most suitable solution for users. Moreover, Taobao uses the LLMs for summarising the comments and opinions of previous buyers. It generates short product reviews for buyers, helping them select the ideal product for their needs. Based on users’ background information and prior research, LLMs will make tailored recommendations. For the same camera, systems may highlight that it is easy to operate for beginners and that it specifies the high resolution for professionals.

Beyond users’ perspectives, Taobao also leverages LLMs to help sellers find target customers based on the descriptions they provide. Without prior sales data, LLMs can still accurately identify the target customer group and provide fairer, more valuable recommendations. Using the LLMs' recommendations system can also address the “Item Cold Start” problem, which occurs when a new product has just entered the market and has no clicks or reviews, making it hard for the system to recommend it.

HKTV Mall

A leader in Hong Kong e-commerce, its LLM application focuses more on improving local users' shopping efficiency and logistics integration. HKTV Mall uses LLMs to develop the “Recipe Guidance”. Users don’t need to search through each ingredient; they only need to specify which dishes they want to make, and the recommendation system will provide everything they need to make them. They even took one step forward, adding the “one-click add to cart” function that adds all necessary ingredients that match the platform's inventory.

To match the habit of HongKongers, usually use both Cantonese and English in conversation. It is one of the special habits of Hongkongers, but it is actually complex for LLMs to read different languages. Therefore, HKTV Mall develops LLMs that can understand commands to provide more accurate results for users.

As mentioned, HKTV Mall tends to improve the shopping efficiency and logistics integration in Hong Kong. There are 2 major features to achieve it.

  1. Purchase Prediction: LLMs will analyze the shopping habits, for example, how long it takes to make another purchase of tissues/shampoo, and set a reminder for them when the time is almost up. It is mainly used in consumer products to improve the customer experience and efficiency.
  2. O2O Data integration: O2O refers to Online-To-Offline. By analyzing users' preferences for place and time, where and when they usually want to pick up. It saved or suggested the usual pickup point and time for customers, which can skip the time-consuming process, improve efficiency, and make integration with logistics easier.
