What is a Recommender System?

A recommender system is a type of machine learning. It takes advantage of artificial intelligence and algorithms to predict and limit the items that are preferred and of interest to users. Most systems make use of users' data in order to provide personalized recommendations. Those data include searching history, liked items and content, purchase history, etc.

Recommender systems help users to explore and navigate more interesting content and items, and save time by helping users to make choices from a wide range of items.

A Brief History of Recommender Systems

The concept of the recommender system arose in the 1970s. At that time, there was a librarian system called Grundy, and it could provide book suggestions to users. The first commercial recommender system emerged in the 1990s, and it was called Tapestry. It was designed for filtering spam and useless information. In the late 1990s, Amazon applied recommender systems and had a successful experience in business. This made recommender systems become a popular trend and encouraged the diversified development of recommender systems.

Our Discovery

We delved into recommender systems, including the mechanisms, advantages, and disadvantages of different recommender systems, future development, and ethical issues of recommender systems by making this website. By learning the theories behind recommendations, we better understand how recommendations are made, what the future trend of making recommendations would be, and what ethical problems we might need to be concerned about. Meanwhile, we discovered that while traditional recommender systems rely heavily on user history, LLM-based ones can provide more context-aware suggestions. Not only did we focus on the theoretical content, but we also searched for practical examples of using large language models, including those from Taobao and HKTV Mall. We have tried them to see how convenient and accurate the recommendations are as well. We found that they are surprisingly accurate in predicting our preferences, and this turns theoretical knowledge into real-life experience.

Apart from theoretical knowledge, we also learned and applied different skills and technologies that are not taught in the lectures and tutorials when we were making our website. For example, we used Bootstrap 5 Responsive Design, and we discovered that using Bootstrap can make the responsive design process easier, and it is more suitable for beginners to create mobile-friendly layouts. Additionally, we tried to add different interactive functions to enhance the users' experience when browsing our website. We added a multi-page navigation and a sidebar for users to quickly locate their interested content. Moreover, we have tried to include an interactive quiz session in one of our webpages for users to check their own understanding, and we found that this makes the website more engaging.
